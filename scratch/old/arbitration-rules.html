ICAMEK Arbitration Rules

The Governing Rules for All Arbitration Proceedings at ICAMEK

Governing Rules

ICAMEK Arbitration Rules 2018

ICAMEK has published Arbitration Rules which govern all arbitration proceedings administered by the Centre. These rules ensure integrity, enforceability, and procedural clarity for all parties wishing to resolve their disputes through ICAMEK.

The ICAMEK Arbitration Rules provide the procedural framework within which all arbitrations are conducted. Parties may refer their disputes to ICAMEK arbitration either through an arbitration agreement or clause in their contract, or by special agreement at the time a dispute arises.

All arbitration proceedings at ICAMEK are conducted strictly in accordance with these published rules, providing parties with the certainty and confidence they require to resolve even the most complex commercial disputes.

Key Highlights of the ICAMEK Arbitration Rules
Number of Arbitrators

Parties can agree on a sole arbitrator or a tribunal of three arbitrators. If parties do not agree, ICAMEK decides based on the complexity of the case and the amount in dispute.

Arbitrator Appointment

The majority of appointments are made by the parties. ICAMEK provides a list of arbitrators for sole arbitrator cases. For three-member tribunals, each side appoints one and ICAMEK appoints the chairperson unless the parties agree otherwise.

Registration Fee

Under the ICAMEK Rules the registration fee amounts to UGX 350,000/=. The advance on costs is calculated by ICAMEK based on the parties' claims and paid in equal shares.

Hearings

A hearing shall be held if requested by a party, or if deemed appropriate by the Arbitral Tribunal. The Tribunal shall, in consultation with the parties, determine the date, time and location of any hearing.

Statement of Claim

The Claimant submits a Statement of Claim including the specific relief sought, material circumstances relied on, and documents relied on. The Respondent submits a corresponding Statement of Defence.

The Award

The final award is rendered by the Arbitral Tribunal. An award is final and binding on the parties when rendered. The Tribunal may decide a separate issue or part of the dispute in a separate award.

International Enforceability

All ICAMEK arbitral awards are enforceable internationally under the New York Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards 1958.

Diversity Policy

Where there are many arbitrators of similar qualifications, ICAMEK's Appointments Committee actively considers diversity of gender, age and national origin.

ICAMEK Arbitration Model Clause

"Any dispute or difference arising out of or in connection with this agreement, including any question regarding its existence, validity or termination, shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration under the Rules of the International Centre for Arbitration and Mediation in Kampala (ICAMEK), which Rules are deemed to be incorporated by reference into this clause."
